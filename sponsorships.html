Sponsorship Opportunities

The Page Society thrives on the support of its members, whether it comes in the form of their attending events, participating in committee work, nominating new members for Page, Page Up and Future Leaders, or providing critical financial support. While membership dues and conference fees go a long way, these only cover a portion of the Society's annual expenses. Moreover, the Page Society has in recent years undertaken a significant increase in its level of programming and support to members. In particular, a great level of resource has gone into producing compelling, industry-leading thought leadership, conducting outreach to leading members of the profession globally, expanding the professional development support for members' own teams, and an increasing number of Page events with Insight Forums and our two major conferences each year. Our development program is an essential part of delivering on the Page mission of strengthening the enterprise leadership role of the CCO and funding the expanding support and programs that our members value.

Diversity & Inclusion
$45,000 Goal

Page D&I work includes the Diverse Future, a week-long executive education program for diverse professionals, the Leaders of Tomorrow, a series of one-day events for young professionals, and resources for Page members that will help them to advance D&I in their own organizations. These programs benefit from both in-kind and financial support.

Thought Leadership Research & Content
$100,000 Goal

This is a key area of investment for Page, with a new report in 2019 that will present Page’s most recent research. Our research is charting a path forward for CCOs as they contend with unprecedented disruption and change. Investment is needed to fund research as well as produce practical content, tools and resources, including case studies, that will help members apply our work within their enterprises. We will build a library of Page member cases, publish posts on topics related to our Thought Leadership report at least once a week and continue to deliver relevant video clips from our events speakers.

The New CCO Podcast
$50,000 Goal

The New CCO podcast showcases member cases, event content and debates/discussions on critical issues for Page members. Just two years old, the podcast has been downloaded 9,000 times and has a five-star rating. We will produce a third season of up to 15 episodes, with topics focused on Page Thought Leadership work.

Globalization
$60,000 Goal

We can improve the value of our network by making it more globally diverse. A more global network prepares members to contribute to the enterprise at a higher level and enables CCOs of major nonU.S. enterprises to contribute to and learn from the network. We will organize up to five full-day events built around our Thought Leadership report where we will present our findings and recommendations.

We plan to develop a new digital strategy that includes transforming our website to make it more useful and engaging for members and other visitors; offering a mobile app that delivers content and other features for members; taking a more sophisticated approach to email and content marketing; and increasing our data analytics capabilities to deliver more personalized content and experiences.

2020 Spring Seminar
$125,000 Goal

A preliminary look at the results of the latest Thought Leadership project will feature member case studies discovered through our research; joint sessions with CEOs and their CCOs discussing their collaboration in delivering transformation in the face of disruption; and peer conversations on critical issues. Additional support would allow us to have access to experts we wouldn’t normally have, including support for speaker travel and honoraria.

Now in its seventh year, the Page Up Annual Conference continues to be the main gathering of its members. Sponsor

Case Study Competition
$20,000 Goal

In alliance with the Institute for Public Relations, Page conducts an annual competition for the writing of original case studies by students enrolled in a school of business, communication or journalism. The competition aims to introduce the practical applications of the core principles that define public relations as a critical function of management and to encourage research that contributes to the profession’s growth and impact. Winners are awarded cash prizes and your support would allow us to keep this important program going.
