Getting The Most Out of Your Page Membership

You get out what you put in, so please take a look around the various ways you can get engaged below. To get started on your journey to engage please contact Mary Elliott, Vice President, Member Engagement, melliott@page.org with any questions. Thanks so much for being part of the Page community.

Our purpose of uniting the world's best communicators to transform business for the better has never been so vital. As we confront new challenges and the increasing speed with which they seem to be arising, Page is geared to make your membership even more valuable in 2019.

We are striving to add real value to your membership by making Page an indispensable resource that makes you a stronger leader. Among other things, we'll be offering more learning and networking programs. More fresh thinking and useful tools you can use every day. And more opportunities to upskill your team. Collaborate: Join A Committee

As a member-driven organization, there are many opportunities to get involved with the work of Page. This is a great way to drive our direction and activities and engage with fellow members as well.

PageConnect: Take your membership to the next level

Regional Events: Connect with other Page members in your region to discuss issues, share content, learn from each other and make new connections by hosting or attending one of these events.

  • Regional Networking Events: Bring together Page members in your region with one of these informal receptions tailored to build community over breakfast, dinner or drinks.
  • Regional Roundtables: Have a topic you want to discuss with your peers? Work with Page staff to host one of these events. Regional roundtables are built to bring members together around specific topics for informal discussion and sharing of best practices.
  • Insight Forum: If you want to take a deep dive into an area of interest, host an Insight Forum. These are full or half-day presentation-style events with speakers, followed by a cocktail reception or dinner.

If you would like to plan your regional event in the US, contact Dhaakira Braswell at dbraswell@page.org

To develop an event that is outside of the US, contact Errick Towns at etowns@page.org.

Digital Events: As Page’s membership continues to expand, take advantage of these opportunities to engage with your peers globally.

  • Webinars: Whether you want a primer on cutting edge research or behind-the-scenes insights about a compelling case study, tune in for our monthly webinars. You’ll have the opportunity to engage directly with our hosts as well as contribute your own insights.
  • Hot Topics: When something happens in the news, who better to discuss its impact with than your fellow Page members? Hot Topic calls are video or conference calls lightly moderated by a host. When you join one, expect to contribute to a high level dialogue about the impact of current events on the communications industry.

Do you have an idea for a digital event topic or want to dive deeper by hosting your own online event? Contact Nicole Graney at ngraney@page.org

Exploring PageConnect Groups


PageConnect Groups are spaces where members with common interests or industries can exchange ideas, share content, discuss issues, solicit advice and make new connections.

We’ll be kicking off with three groups: Societal Value, Financial Services, and Leadership.

Corporate Purpose & Societal Value

Group Leader: Jin Montesano, LIXIL

Societal value creation is becoming a corporate imperative and a central responsibility of the CCO. In this group we explore leading thinking and approaches to developing corporate purpose and creating societal value.




Join Group

Financial Services

Group Leader: Jen Prosek, Prosek Partners

Navigating a turbulent economy and communicating in a regulated industry require specialized approaches to communication. This group convenes Page members in and around Financial Services to discuss common challenges and address emerging issues and best practices.


Join Group

Leadership

Group Leader: Roger Bolton, Page

CCOs today work alongside the CEO and across the C-Suite to build corporate character and authentic stakeholder engagement. This brings the CCO into organizational management issues across brand, culture, policy and operations. This group explores new insights and approaches to becoming a stronger, more influential leader.
