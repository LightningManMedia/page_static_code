Getting Engaged

Opportunities for You to Get the Most Out of Your Page Membership

Our purpose of uniting the world's best communicators to transform business for the better has never been so vital. As we confront new challenges and the increasing speed with which they seem to be arising, Page is geared to make your membership even more valuable in 2019.

We are striving to add real value to your membership by making Page an indispensable resource that makes you a stronger leader. Among other things, we'll be offering more learning and networking programs. More fresh thinking and useful tools you can use every day. And more opportunities to upskill your team. You get out what you put in, so please take a look around the various ways you can get engaged below. To get started on your journey to engage please contact Mary Elliott, Vice President, Member Engagement, melliott@page.org with any questions. Thanks so much for being part of the Page community.

Exploring PageConnect Groups

PageConnect Groups: As the Page community grows, it becomes more important to enable the intimate peer exchanges that make Page so uniquely valuable. PageConnect Groups are member-only affinity groups organized by topics of interest or industry sectors. Their purpose is to bring Page members together in new ways to exchange ideas, share content, solicit advice, make new connections and learn from each other. Currently, there are three groups: Corporate Purpose & Societal Value, Financial Services and Leadership. A fourth group on Culture will be added in the next 30 days.

For more information about PageConnect Groups, please contact our community manager, Natalie Martinez.

Financial Services

Group Leader: Jen Prosek, Prosek Partners

Navigating a turbulent economy and communicating in a regulated industry require specialized approaches to communication. This group convenes Page members in and around Financial Services to discuss common challenges and address emerging issues and best practices.
